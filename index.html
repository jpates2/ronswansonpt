FAQs

Here are some of our FAQs. Get in touch with the form below if you have any other questions.

Can I try your services before committing to a package?

Absolutely! We offer a free 30-minute trial session/consultation for those interested in signing up and experiencing our gym. It's a great opportunity to meet the trainer, tour the facilities, and get a taste of what our training sessions are like.

Do I need to have previous fitness experience to train with you?

Not at all! We welcome individuals of all fitness levels, including beginners. Our trainers specialize in tailoring workouts to your current abilities and gradually progressing from there.

What if I need to cancel or reschedule a session?

Life happens, and we understand that. We have a flexible cancellation policy, and we'll work with you to reschedule sessions when necessary. We appreciate advance notice whenever possible to accommodate your needs and ensure consistent progress toward your goals.

What types of workouts do you offer?

We offer a wide range of workouts tailored to your needs and preferences. These include strength training, cardio workouts, flexibility and mobility exercises, as well as sport-specific training. We'll design a program that aligns with your goals and interests.

Is it recommended to do other exercise outside of the PT sessions?

Yes, it's highly recommended! While our personal training sessions provide focused guidance and structure, incorporating additional solo exercise into your routine can enhance your progress. However, it's also crucial to remember that rest days are essential for recovery and preventing overtraining. Our trainers will help you strike the right balance between structured sessions and independent workouts to optimize your results while maintaining your overall well-being.

How do I get started with your personal training services?

To get started, simply contact us through our website or by phone to schedule an initial consultation. During this session, we'll discuss your fitness goals, assess your current fitness level, and determine the best training plan for you.
